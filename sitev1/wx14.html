A note on Heat Index/Windchill/Humidex: These are all technically-defined measures of what the air actually feels like on one's skin - The Heat Index is useful for factoring in the extra "warming effect" of high relative humidity in warm air, but is only vaild above 27°C with relative humidity >40%; Windchill obviously factors in the effect of the wind, and is generally only defined for temperatures below ~10°C; Humidex is always defined, and should be thought of like the heat index in that it factors in the effect of humidity.
